Could you take 5 minutes to share details of your participatory AI projects for a new case book: showcasing inclusive public engagement on AI from across the world?

Alongside the UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance this July we’re planning to showcase both methods and messages from participatory projects on AI through an interactive case book on the PAIRS website, and a stand at the heart of the AI for Good Expo in Geneva: putting public voices on AI in front of thousands of conference attendees.

<!--more-->

The Public AI Voice and Engagement (PAVE) Case Book is inviting nominations or full submission of short case studies describing the ‘What’, ‘Who’, ‘How’ and ‘What was said?’ from participatory projects focussed on AI development and governance that have taken place in recent years.

Whether your work has already featured in a PAIRS talk, or you have been wanting to see it gain a wider audience, please take a few moments to add a submission via the [simple online form](https://forms.fillout.com/t/u7vQQNPRhpus).

We are also interested in hearing about national participatory initiatives on AI. If there has been a national citizen deliberation, or an inclusive public dialogue focussed on aspects of AI in your country, nominate it for the case book through the form.

The PAVE team will curate a selection of cases to include in a 9m^2 stand at the AI for Good Expo in Geneva from 7th - 10th July, and we will publish a full dataset of submitted cases soon after.

Nominations deadline: 5th June 2026. [Nominate a case here](https://pave.pairs.site).
